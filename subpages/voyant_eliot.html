Word Cloud

The Word Cloud highlights term frequencies and thematic significance in T.S. Eliot's works:

  • Time: Prominent in The Waste Land and The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock, reflecting existential angst and preoccupations with temporality. Minimal in La Figlia che Piange, where the focus is more on immediate imagery.
  • Eyes: Peaks in The Hollow Men, symbolizing both fear of being judged (others’ gaze) and blindness to truth.
  • Wind: Strongly used in A Song for Simeon as a chaotic force bringing death. In The Hollow Men, it symbolizes emptiness.
  • Dead: Most frequent in The Hollow Men and The Waste Land, emphasizing mortality and spiritual decay. In The Waste Land, "death" resonates hopelessness.

Term Frequencies

Contextual observations reveal deeper insights into term usage across poems:

  • Words like "water" and "mountains" in The Waste Land reflect despair (drought and absence of water) and potential renewal (water as nourishment).
  • In contrast, words like "pray" and "hope" in Ash Wednesday suggest a more uplifting spiritual tone.

Word Trends

Word trends reveal how T.S. Eliot’s recurring themes vary across texts:

  • The Waste Land and The Hollow Men focus on decay and renewal.
  • Ash Wednesday and A Song for Simeon emphasize hope and spiritual awakening.

VIVIENNE HAIGH-WOOD ELIOT'S SHORT STORIES

This corpus tends to compare and analyze Vivienne’s short stories, which were written mostly in her numerous diaries. Unlike the unique words used in her poems, the collection of short stories is composed of solely 5.8% unique words.

There are 18 short stories in the corpus, the longest being The Paralysed Woman, and the shortest Telephone Conversation. The variation in length highlights her flexibility in storytelling, ranging from brief, impactful pieces to more developed narratives.

The most used word across her short stories is the name of the main character, "Sibylla." The second most frequent word is "said," indicating dialogue-heavy content. This aligns with the nature of fiction, where character interactions drive the narrative. It may also suggest simplicity and clarity in her storytelling.
